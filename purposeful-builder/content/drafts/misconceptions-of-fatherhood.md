# The Misconceptions of Fatherhood

*This draft has been fully rewritten to faithfully match the raw, unfiltered points you made in your audio. No political correctness—just your exact thoughts on biology, design, single mothers celebrating themselves on Father's Day, and the need for men in a child's life.*

---

There is a common, blasphemous thought that has been going around the world, and even though people subscribe to it, it doesn’t make it true: the idea that what you identify as, or the functions you perform, makes you that thing.

For a man, for example, there is a biological and psychological framework that makes him a man. If you don't pass through those frameworks, you cannot necessarily call yourself a man, even if you identify as one. The same happens for a woman. Even the biological makeup of a man and a woman is obviously different. The structural framework, the hips of a woman, differentiates her from a man. So even if a man speaks like a woman, acts like a woman, and does the things a woman does (aside from the biological functions), it doesn’t necessarily make him a woman.

This goes beyond roles. It goes to **design and purpose**.

God created man first, and being the first, He made him the foundation of families, societies, and communities. That is the law God put in place. So, if a woman provides for or takes care of the family in the absence of a man, it does not make her a man. These things you become by *design*, not by the *roles* you play.

### Design Before Functionality

God created man in His image and likeness. **His first design was before functionality.** 

There are people who are designed in the image of God who do not function like God. There are Christians who have received the Holy Spirit but haven't started functioning like God. And there are people who don't have the image of God who try to do good things—but according to Jesus, doing good things does not mean that image has come inside of you. 

Performing the duties of a man does not make you a man.

The design of God concerning man does not change just because a man becomes incompetent in handling his duties. For example, by biological design, a man releases sperm, and a woman has a fertilized egg, they mate, and we have a child. If a man does not release sperm, it doesn’t reduce him as a man. If a woman does not give birth to children, it doesn’t reduce her as a woman. It simply means there is a malfunction, but neither can produce what the other does. Those two things are exclusive to them by design.

If a woman is primed to nurture a baby, and she dies at childbirth, and the man takes on the responsibility—finding a way to provide milk and nurturing the child—he cannot wake up tomorrow and call himself the *mother* of the child. He is the father.

The same way, if a man dies, and the woman takes on the responsibility of providing for the child, paying bills, and all that—it does not make her the father. Calling her a father reduces men to merely the things they *do*. It reduces a man to just provision. But fatherhood is beyond function.

### Stop Insulting Fathers on Father’s Day

This brings us to Father's Day.

There is a trend of single mothers celebrating themselves on Father's Day, saying they are the fathers. But let’s be clear: a woman takes care of her children, and they probably turn out well. But I know that it is very rare to see sons, especially, turn out truly well when raised exclusively by a woman. They might do well financially or academically, but to be a healthy, whole man, you can only turn out well as a man if you are raised by a man.

People celebrate their mothers on Father's Day because they played the role of their fathers, or because their biological father was a deadbeat or emotionally absent. They have not been properly trained. 

If your biological father was not present in your life, on Father's Day, you can choose not to post him. It is wisdom not to write anything about him on that day. **But you can celebrate the male figures who played fatherly roles in your life.**

Beyond providing and paying bills, there is a transfer of identity that men transfer to their children. That thing cannot be done by a woman. This is why identity crises are prevalent in children raised by single mothers, especially when the single mother has issues with the father. She transfers her hatred of the man to the children, and they grow up hating their fathers. Even in divorce, you see mothers intentionally manipulating children to hate their fathers. 

You always hear women say, "If your father was here, he wouldn't have done this. These are your father's values." Even in his absence, the woman is still communicating the value the man holds to the children!

Father's Day is supposed to be for **men, males.** Not women who played the role of fathers. To do that is to reduce men to mere duties.

On Mother's Day, we celebrate mothers. We don't celebrate fathers who were there when their wives died or left them for another man. We don't do that. It is a sacred day for women who are mothers. When I see men or women celebrating their mothers on Father's Day, I think there is something wrong with their perception of what a father is.

### A Message for Single Mothers

Fathers are a foundation. That position is given to them by God. And it's not because they did anything to earn it—when God made them foundations, they hadn't even done anything on earth yet. By divine design, it is what it is, and people should learn to respect fatherhood.

Mothers who cause these problems—how you train your child determines how they perceive fathers. If your husband is a deadbeat, was violent or abusive to you, and you begin to raise boys or children, **find a father figure to oversee them.**

People don't know what a father connotes. They think what their mothers did for them can quantify what a father can do.

I grew up early without a father. I appreciate the work my mother did in my life. She began to play roles my father could no longer play because he wasn't there. But I needed men to grow up. So on Father's Day, I celebrate my father—a posthumous celebration—and then I celebrate the men God has used to raise me. Because those are the men who will speak identity to you. 

It is beyond having money. There are many men who have money but can't build solid families, can't manage a woman, can't manage homes. But they provide. You see "mummy's boys" always leaning into what their mother says over their own families, exalting their mothers' welfare over their wives' welfare, forgetting their wife became a priority the minute they got married. We need to go back to authentic fatherhood.

Any man or woman who is trained by a proper man cannot relegate men to just provision. So people should stop doing that thing they are doing. Father's Day is for fathers.

There should be somebody in your life who plays the role of a father, who is biologically a male. Don't celebrate your mothers on Father's Day. You are insulting us.

Let children observe fatherhood. Let them grow up with zero hatred for their fathers. Tell them their father didn't do well because he didn't know well. Project a good image. Shield them from that hatred. I had a personal good image of my father because I can still remember memories of him. If my parents had issues, they contained it. Let children grow up with good memories of their father. 

If they had bad fathers who inflicted pain, they don't need to grow up hating men. Shield them from that. Intentionally navigate their path to meet with good men who reflect good fatherhood to them, so they can have a good image of fatherhood. 

When they understand the value of fatherhood, they will not come on Father's Day and celebrate you. It is not wisdom for your children to grow up and celebrate you on a day that is not meant for you. Tell them you are doing your work as a mother, but there is a man who can compensate for the absence of their father, and tell them the importance of fatherhood. In school, find men they can submit to. It helps build discipline, it grounds them in their identity, and it helps them see what a man should be.

You cannot be a father, no matter what you try to do for your kids. There is no way you can be one. Let children observe fatherhood. Let them grow up with zero hatred for their father.
